About

I'm a Machine Learning Engineer at EdgeTier in Dublin, where I build AI systems that analyse every customer interaction in real-time. My work spans LLM scaling, agentic products built on MCP, and CI/CD infrastructure for ML microservices.

My PhD in Statistics from Maynooth University focused on acoustic machine learning for biodiversity monitoring — using deep learning and signal processing to detect and classify wildlife from passive audio recordings. My research was supervised by Prof. Andrew Parnell and Prof. Ian Donohue, and resulted in publications in PeerJ, Ecological Informatics, and a Springer book chapter.

Before the PhD, I studied Mathematics at Trinity College Dublin, graduating with First Class Honours and a Gold Medal. I also contributed to JASP, a free open-source statistics package, at the University of Amsterdam.

Outside work: film, podcasts, reading, cooking.
