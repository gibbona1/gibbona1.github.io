About

I'm a Machine Learning Engineer at EdgeTier in Dublin, building AI systems that analyse every customer interaction in real-time. My work spans LLM scaling, agentic products built on MCP, and CI/CD infrastructure for ML microservices.

My PhD in Statistics from Maynooth University focused on acoustic machine learning for biodiversity — using deep learning and signal processing to detect and classify wildlife from passive audio recordings. I collaborated across ecology, statistics, and software engineering, publishing in PeerJ, Ecological Informatics, and Springer.

Before the PhD, I studied Mathematics at Trinity College Dublin, graduating with First Class Honours and a Gold Medal. I also contributed to the open-source statistics package JASP at the University of Amsterdam.

Outside of work: film, podcasts, reading, and cooking.
